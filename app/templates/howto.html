How it works

Create a New Track

Navigate to the New Track page. Enter the Name of Track (i.e. Single Ladies) and Original Artist (i.e. Beyonc√©). These two fields will help other users looking to collaborate on specific songs find your track. Selecting Hidden will prevent your track from turning up when searched. However, it will still be accessible from your account and by anyone with the URL. This feature is useful for private collaborations. Finally, to create the track you can either record directly from your browser by hitting Add Recording for New Track or you can upload a music file to start the track by selecting Upload Audio to Start Track

If you select Add Recording for New Track you will hear a 3 second countdown, after which the recording will begin. Once you are done recording, select Add Recording for New Track again to stop recording. The following waveform will then be displayed. Hit Play to play the recording and decide if you are satisfied with it. If you are not, you many Delete Recording and try again, or Save Recording as a new track.

Adding Harmonies

Note: you must be logged in to add harmonies
Navigate to a track page. Click to seek to the location where you want to start recording from. You can also just record from the beginning of the track. Hit Start/Stop Recording. You will be given a 3 second countdown and then recording will begin at the location of the trackbar. Headphones and a mic are strongly suggested for recording to avoid feedback. To finish recording hit Start/Stop Recording.

After you are finished recording, the waveform will then be displayed. Play the recording and decide if you are satisfied with it. If you are not, you many Delete Recording and try again, or Save Recording to the track as an additional layer.

If you decide to save the harmony, the following prompt will be displayed. Enter a descriptive name for the harmony and hit Save.

Editing Tracks

Note: you must be logged in to edit tracks
Navigate to a track page. All layers can be muted, whether or not you are logged in. To Delete Layer , if you are the owner of the track, you can delete any layer associated with the track. If you are not the creator of the track, you can only delete your own layers. In the following example, since the track creator is "Noah Zweben", and I am logged in as itchynose27, I am only able to delete the low chorus harmony layer because I recorded it. This feature ensures that the original creator of a track has the final say on the harmonies included in the track.
Please note that deleting layers and tracks cannot be undone

If you are the creator of a track, you may also delete the entire track.
